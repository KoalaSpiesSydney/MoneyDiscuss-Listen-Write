Wise Old Adages about Money

Part 1: Discuss meanings and opinions. Part 2: Match speakers’ attitudes.

PART 1

Discuss: What do they mean? Which do you agree with?

1. “Money doesn’t grow on trees.”
2. “A penny saved is a penny earned.”
3. “Time is money.”
4. “The best things in life are free.”
5. “You have to spend money to make money.”
6. “Neither a borrower nor a lender be.”
7. “Easy come, easy go.”
8. “Fool and his money are soon parted.”
9. “Money talks.”
10. “Cash is king.”
Tip: Give an example from your language. Which adages seem to contradict each other? Which fit modern life best?

PART 2

In IELTS/CAE Part 3 and PTE, your understanding of attitude and opinion is tested. Words differ, but the gist matters most. Listen and match two adages to each audio.

Tip: Read the main question only; make brief notes while listening.

Right, listen—I've been a sparkie for thirty years, and I’ll tell you what keeps the lights on, straight deals. You finish a job, you get paid on the spot, no mucking about with promises or invoices to be paid in a few months. If I can’t fold it or stash it in my wallet, it’s not worth my time. Another tip, watch every screw, every litre of fuel, every offcut—because it, all, adds up. I’ve seen lads lose a week’s wages just by not checking the docket. Me? I tally every bit. That’s how you stay ahead. You treat every dollar like it’s the last one you’ll ever see.

Look, I keep things simple. I don’t believe in, lending out money. I’ve seen too many friendships, lost trying to help a friend out or chasing some short-term fix. It’s messy, and it rarely ends well. But neither do I ever borrow from friends, family, or even the bank. If I can’t afford to invest at that time, I wait. And when it comes to gains? I don’t celebrate too early. Markets move fast—especially in crypto. You might wake up rich and go to bed broke. So, I take profit when it’s there, and I don’t get attached. If something comes easy, I don’t assume it’ll stick around. That’s how I stay ahead, by keeping my head clear, and my wallet under control.
